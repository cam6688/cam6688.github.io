WHO AM I?

Hello! My name is Cameron and welcome to my portfolio! As an aspiring creator, I am passionate about making all sorts of things - from animated short films to web applications, and more! Please look at my work, and contact me if you have any questions :)

Programmer

As a Computer Science major at UC Berkeley, I love to code! Most of my projects involve backend implementation, but I enjoy developing frontend UI in my personal projects. I also love creating video games (especially choose your own adventure visual novels) and hope to make more in upcoming years.

Art and Animation

I am a double major in Art Practice at UC Berkeley, with a focus in graphics and animation. My favorite mediums and programs include Procreate, CLIP Studio Paint, oil paint and charcoal. I'm extremely passionate about creating animated short films, and have created 3 in the past year. My next short, Mirage, is releasing in May 2021.

Graphic Design, Makeup, Photography

On the side, I like doing photography, videography, graphic design and makeup artistry. These are all skills I've independently taught myself since starting college. I plan to start a Twitch channel this year to develop and showcase these skills, and hopefully teach others in the future.

Pacman

PythonAIReinforcement LearningInference

In my Introduction to Artifical Intelligence class, we did 4 projects that built upon the topics of AI by implementing them into a Pacman game. We first utilized search algorithms to teach Pacman how to eat all the pellets on a board, then used reinforcment learning and reward assignments to prevent Pacman from being eaten by ghosts. We eventually made our Pacman predict ghost locations, and finally had Pacman successfully win all games with scores over 1000, in a full implementation of the game's rules. You can learn more about the projects that I did for this class from the course website here.

Handwriting Classifier

PythonMachine Learning

I built an artificial neural network to classify numbers by training off of the MNIST handwritten digit database. By implementing a linear regression model to train the data, I gradually added more layers to create a complex network that could have a 97% or greater test accuracy. Finally, I redesigned the network by adding extra layers and training to classify the language that words were written in (English, Spanish, Finnish, Dutch, and Polish), which had over 89% test accuracy.
